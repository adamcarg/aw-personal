Test drive tips

Taking a car for a test drive can be fun — but what it needs to be is informative. If you're not sure what to look for, bring along someone who knows cars. Just don't leave it entirely to them. You're buying this car for yourself.

New or used?

New cars don't usually break down during a test drive — what you're assessing is whether the design suits your needs.

For used cars, check the service book — has it been maintained punctually? It's worth getting a professional mechanic to inspect it before you discuss price. They'll check for rust, oil leaks, worn clutch and brakes, and transmission issues.

Handing over your licence

Leave the seller something of value before you drive off — your licence or the keys to your current car.

Make sure you both have each other's contact details, and check whether you'll be covered by the car's insurance policy while you're driving it.

How long and where?

Allow at least half an hour. Spend five minutes setting up the seat — if you're re-adjusting again at the end, it's probably not the right car.

Drive where you actually drive. Freeway commute? Find a highway. Mostly suburbs? Find roundabouts and stop signs.

On the road
Are comfort and convenience features easy to reach?
Can you pair your phone via Bluetooth?
Does the climate control work?
Does the car ride nicely and feel quiet?
Do you feel confident steering into corners?
How are the brakes and overall performance?
Does it go well enough for your needs?

If the answer to all of these is yes — you're on to a winner.
